# ⚖️ Module 7 — AI & the World: Ethics, Safety & the Future

> **📋 Parent note:** No coding here — just some of the most important conversations of your child's generation. There are no perfect answers, and that's the point. Let your child form opinions and defend them. These discussions build the judgment that will matter far more than any specific tool.

## Lesson 7.1 — Fairness, Privacy & Your Data

### 🧠 The Big Idea
Two big responsibilities come with AI:

- **Fairness:** As you learned in Module 3, biased data makes unfair AI. When AI helps decide things like loans, jobs, or who police investigate, unfairness can really hurt people. Fixing it is a serious job that real engineers work on.
- **Privacy:** AI runs on data — *your* data. Every search, like, and message can be used to train systems or predict your behavior. Being smart about what you share is a superpower.

### 💬 Think About It
- A free app gives you cool AI features in exchange for your data. Is that a fair trade? When is it *not*?
- Should there be things AI is simply *not allowed* to decide about people?

## Lesson 7.2 — Deepfakes & Misinformation

### 🧠 The Big Idea
Generative AI can create fake photos, fake voices, and fake videos of real people doing things they never did — called **deepfakes.** This makes a skill called **critical thinking** more important than ever.

Your defense kit:
- **Consider the source.** Who posted this, and why?
- **Look for confirmation.** Do trusted, independent places report the same thing?
- **Be suspicious of strong emotions.** Fake content is often designed to make you instantly angry or scared so you share before thinking.
- **When in doubt, don't share.**

### 🎮 Do It: Spot the Fake
With a parent, look at examples of AI-generated images (lots of "AI vs. real" quizzes exist online). Hunt for clues — weird hands, garbled text, impossible reflections. Then discuss: the clues are disappearing fast as AI improves. So what do we rely on *then*? (Answer: sources and verification, not just our eyes.)

### 💬 Think About It
- If you can't always trust what you see, what *can* you trust?

## Lesson 7.3 — Jobs, the Future & AI Safety

### 🧠 The Big Idea
AI will change the world your generation grows up into:
- Some jobs will fade; many new ones will appear (a lot of today's AI jobs didn't exist 10 years ago).
- The best skills to build are the ones AI is *worst* at: creativity, judgment, empathy, asking good questions, and working with people.
- Scientists also work on **AI safety** — making sure powerful AI systems are reliable, controllable, and actually helpful to humanity. It's one of the most interesting frontiers you could grow into.

### 💬 Think About It
- What's a job you'd love that AI *couldn't* easily do?
- If you were in charge of the world's AI rules, what would be your #1 rule?

### 🏆 Module 7 Check
You can now think critically about fairness, privacy, fakes, and the future. You're not just AI-skilled — you're AI-*wise.* That's rare, even among adults. 🦉
