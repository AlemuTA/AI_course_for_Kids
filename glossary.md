# 📚 Glossary — Your AI Dictionary

- **AI (Artificial Intelligence):** Machines doing tasks that normally need human-like thinking.
- **Algorithm:** A step-by-step set of instructions to solve a problem.
- **Bias:** Unfairness an AI learns from one-sided or flawed data.
- **Data:** Information a computer can use to learn (images, text, numbers, sound).
- **Deep Learning:** Machine learning using neural networks with many layers.
- **Deepfake:** AI-generated fake image, audio, or video of a real person.
- **Epoch:** One full pass through the training data while learning.
- **Feature:** One measurable property of your data (e.g., a dog's size).
- **Generative AI:** AI that creates new content — text, images, music, video.
- **Hallucination:** When an AI states false information confidently.
- **Label:** The "correct answer" attached to a training example.
- **LLM (Large Language Model):** A text AI (like ChatGPT/Claude) that predicts the next word.
- **Machine Learning (ML):** AI that learns patterns from examples instead of hand-written rules.
- **Model:** The pattern-finder produced by training.
- **Neural Network:** AI loosely inspired by brain cells, made of connected "neurons."
- **Overfitting:** When a model memorizes its training data instead of learning the real pattern.
- **Prompt:** The instruction you give an AI.
- **Reinforcement Learning:** Learning through trial, error, and rewards.
- **Supervised Learning:** Learning from labeled examples.
- **Training:** The process of teaching a model from data.
- **Unsupervised Learning:** Finding hidden groups in unlabeled data.
- **Weight:** A "volume knob" that controls how much an input matters to a neuron.
