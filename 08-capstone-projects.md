# 🏗️ Module 8 — Capstone Projects

> **📋 Parent note:** This is where it all comes together. Your child picks one project and builds it over a week or two, combining skills from the whole course. Celebrate the finished result — show it to family, screen-record it, frame it as a real achievement. Pride in a finished build is what turns a learner into a maker.

Pick **one** (or do several over time). Each uses tools you already know.

### 🎯 Project A — The Smart Sorter
Build a Teachable Machine model that sorts something *you* care about — trading cards by type, recycling vs. trash, your pets apart, different hand-drawn shapes. Push it to be as accurate as you can by improving your data. **Write up:** what made it better, what fooled it.

### 🎯 Project B — The AI Story Studio
Use a chatbot (with a parent) to co-write an illustrated short story. *You* are the director: you write the prompts, choose what's good, and rewrite what isn't. Generate a few images to go with it. **The skill:** directing AI with great prompts while keeping *your* creative vision in charge.

### 🎯 Project C — The Custom Chatbot Character
Using a chatbot (with a parent), design a character with a personality — say, a pirate who only speaks in riddles — by writing detailed instructions. Test it, refine the instructions, and "interview" your creation. **The skill:** advanced prompting and seeing how instructions shape behavior.

### 🎯 Project D — The Phone App
With MIT App Inventor, finish a complete AI app (the talking camera from Module 5, or your own idea). **The skill:** building a full, working pipeline from input to AI to output.

### 🎯 Project E — The Teacher
The ultimate test of understanding: **teach someone else.** Make a short slideshow or video explaining how AI learns, using your own analogies. If you can teach it, you truly know it. (This is also how real experts level up.)
